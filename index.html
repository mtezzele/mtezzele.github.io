About
I am a Ph.D. student in Mathematical Analysis, Modelling, and Applications at International School of Advanced Studies (SISSA), Trieste, Italy, where I am part of the SISSA mathLab group.
My industrial Ph.D. grant is sponsored by Fincantieri S.p.A. (see the project here). The topic is the structural optimization of a passenger ship during the design step through parametric techniques and computational reduction.

In the last years I focused on the development of non-intrusive data-driven reduced order methods (ROMs) such as proper orthogonal decomposition with interpolation (PODI) and dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), with applications in naval engineering and biomedical engineering. This resulted in the creation of several scientific python packages. Moreover I coupled ROMs with reduction in parameter space using active subspaces (AS) and other non-linear techniques such as local linear embedding (LLE).

Now I am working on structural optimization and shape design optimization using ROMs and non-linear reduction of parameter space with kernel-based AS and artificial neural networks.
